A mobile app for international students by offering resources, support, and community-building features. The app's purpose is to support students in overcoming the obstacles of studying abroad and maximizing their experience in a foreign country.

Overview

The Problem

International students often struggle to find the resources and support they need to succeed in a new country. They face a variety of challenges, including navigating a new academic and cultural environment, and finding social connections.

The Solution

A mobile app that can provide international students with an all-inclusive and accessible resources, support, and community-building opportunities to help them successfully navigate the challenges of studying abroad and make the most of their time in a new country.

Why create an app for International Students?

  • After gaining insight into what motivated students to study abroad, significant pain points were identified which was crucial in helping develop a design solution that would address those needs. .
  • Research showed that International students struggled with adapting to a new culture.
  • Finding housing and financial support is a difficult process especially for International students.
  • International students need more resources and support when it comes to accessing social services.
  • All of the users interviewed felt that isolation and loneliness was a major struggle they faced when integrating into a new country.

User Research

Interview Details

To further investigate these struggles / we conducted in-depth one-on-one interviews to collect qualitative data to gain insights into the students’ experiences and needs. Of the 5 participants interviewed, 2 came from the Philippines, 1 from Germany, and 2 from Canada who chose to study abroad in France. These participants all represented a diverse range of experiences and perspectives.

  1. The language barriers when integrating into a new country and school.
  2. Finding a place to live and maintaining expenses are difficult.
  3. Afraid of socializing and building a community to escape isolation.

Results

After gaining insight into what motivated students to study abroad, we identified significant pain points which would be crucial in helping us develop a design solution that would address those needs. Some common themes that emerged were: adapting to a new culture, finding housing, finances, and accessing social support and services.

“As for language, once got there I didn’t know anything. It was hard to make friends.”

“Finding a place to live was my number one struggle.”

“I wish I had done more if I wasn't so scared of pursuing new connections.”

Affinity Diagram

The key points that were found during our user interviews were sorted into categories to determine what should be the main focus on our app.

Based on the number of points in each category, we determined which topics should be focused on. In this case we prioritized adapting to a new culture, finding housing, finances, and accessing social support and services.

User Persona

Based on the research, I developed the user persona "Linh Tran" to target the user.

As a result, we were able to validate our user persona in the form of Linh Tran. A 24-year old single female from Vietnam who is pursuing her masters degree in Business Administration at the U of T. She is resourceful and driven and excited about getting a top-notch education in a multicultural country. Though she feels overwhelmed looking for affordable housing and struggling with the cold weather, she is determined to succeed / and build a career in her new country.

Definition and Ideation

User Insight Statement

International students need to have access to a support network because despite being surrounded by many people, they feel lonely and disconnected due to cultural differences and language barriers.

Problem Statement

Research and user interviews uncovered that many international students struggle to navigate the unfamiliar culture, find social connections, and access resources for various needs. Therefore, providing a centralized platform that offers both mentorship and peer support programs, as well as a directory of resources for job search, housing, legal aid, and mental health support, could greatly benefit international students in their transition and help them succeed in their academic and personal life.

I Like, I Wish, What If?

To organize the feedback that was gathered from the user interviews, the “I like, I Wish, What If” method was used to display statements that could help develop what elements would be important for creating a prototype for the travel app.

These statements would allow me to understand what users currently like, their desires, and future possibilities for an International Student App.

Prioritization Method

The statements were then sorted to determine the prioritization of certain elements and features that the travel app should be focused on.

Value Proposition Statement

This mobile app designed for international students in Canada offers a comprehensive and inclusive solution to many of the common challenges faced by individuals from different countries studying in a new environment. It provides a variety of resources such as a helpline for social support, housing options, job postings, events, and campus activities to ensure that students have access to the information they need to make the most of their time in Canada. In addition, the app also provides mental health resources, access to community and other international students, and a translator to help with language barriers. This app is a valuable tool for students seeking to integrate into Canadian society and make the most of their study abroad experience.

User Testing

After designing the prototype, I conducted four Usability Tests to gain feedback for SomeBuddy. The three tasks that the users had to perform were:

  1. Signing up with email.
  2. Verifying their accounts.
  3. Go to "Find a connection" and send a message in the Community Chat.
